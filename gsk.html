GSK - Automation Engineering Internship

Description

In August 2021 I finished my year-long internship as an Automation Engineer at GlaxoSmithKline, Barnard Castle. I was awarded The Engineering Development Trust Industrial Cadets Platinum Leaders Award for demonstrating key professional skills and engaging in STEM outreach and volunteering during my placement. I learnt to use state-of-the-art digital manufacturing and automation systems, becoming proficient in working with automation hardware and software. My key roles included: 1. Validating the communication interface between the manufacturing-execution-system and Siemens PCS7 distributed control system. 2. Wrote a site-wide standard operating procedure to introduce new ways of working with OPC server software (Kepware) 3. Supporting validation projects within the new aseptic manufacturing facility and network migrations on site. Extracurricular activities helped me grow as an individual and strengthened my abilities as an engineer, leader and communicator: 1. Took up a six-week Python course for professionals at the Teesside University, UK, to refresh and deepen my skills in Python. 2. Regularly volunteered to promote careers in STEM to students, from middle school to university, by delivering presentations, judging on the panel of a sustainable design competition, and more.
